# Job Hunt Stack: 7-Step Workflow Guide
### From First Search to Offer Accepted

---

## Overview

This workflow takes you from zero to employed. Each step builds on the previous one.

```
Step 1: Source > Step 2: Build > Step 3: Template > Step 4: Refine > Step 5: Scan > Step 6: Apply > Step 7: Expand
```

---

## Step 1: Source Leads via Teal / Simplify Jobs

**Agent**: TSA J Search

Start by using job aggregator platforms to cast a wide net:

- **Teal** (tealhq.com): AI-powered job tracking and resume matching
- **Simplify Jobs** (simplify.jobs): One-click batch applications with saved profiles
- **LinkedIn Jobs**: Filter by remote, salary, experience level
- **Indeed / Glassdoor**: Broad coverage with company reviews

Save 20-30 leads before moving to Step 2. At this stage, quantity matters more than precision.

---

## Step 2: Create / Refine Your Resume by Searching Jobs

**Agent**: TSA Port (with TSA J Search support)

1. Read through 10-15 job descriptions in your target role
2. Extract the most common keywords, skills, and qualifications
3. Cross-reference against your actual experience
4. Build a **master resume** broadly optimized for your target role category

Benchmark: Your resume should pass ATS screening for 70%+ of roles in your target category without customization.

---

## Step 3: Download Resume as Template for Master ATS List

**Agent**: TSA J Leads (with TSA Port support)

Your master resume is now your template. Extract from it:

1. Every ATS keyword you've included
2. Keywords you're missing that appeared frequently in job descriptions
3. Keywords you could add if you slightly reframe existing experience

Compile these into a **Master ATS List**:

| Your Skill / Experience | ATS Keyword(s) | Frequency in JDs | In Master Resume? |
|------------------------|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Led cross-functional teams | Cross-functional leadership, team management | High | Yes |
| Used Jira for sprint planning | Jira, Agile, sprint planning, Scrum | High | Yes |
| Managed project budgets | Budget management, cost control, P&L | Medium | No - add |

This list becomes your cheat sheet for every future resume customization.

---

## Step 4: Refine Custom Resumes and Cover Letters from Master ATS List

**Agent**: TSA Port (triggered by TSA J Leads Top 3 selection)

1. TSA J Leads selects the **Top 3 priority leads** from the pipeline
2. For each lead, TSA Port creates a tailored resume and cover letter
3. Both documents follow the matched pair naming convention
4. TSA J Leads logs the filenames in the tracker Notes column

As you customize, update the Master ATS List. Every application makes it more accurate, which makes future applications faster.

---

## Step 5: Scan All Leads for Personal Preferences Using Color Scheme

**Agent**: TSA J Leads

With your Master ATS List complete, scan every lead against your personal preferences:

| Color | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **Green** | Strong match: role, culture, comp, values all align | Apply immediately |
| **Yellow** | Decent match: most criteria met, minor concerns | Apply if time permits |
| **Red** | Dead or rejected: expired, ghosted, or misaligned | Archive |
| **Blue** | Accepted offer | Celebrate |
| No color | Not yet triaged | Needs review |

This step is about intentionality. Color coding makes the decision visual and fast.

---

## Step 6: Mass Apply for All Relevant Leads

**Agent**: All agents coordinated by TSA Flo

1. **Green leads first**: Apply to every green lead with tailored materials
2. **Yellow leads next**: Apply if you have capacity
3. **Batch applications**: Platforms like Simplify allow one-click batch applying, but ALWAYS log every application in the tracker

TSA Flo schedules application sessions (2-3 PDS blocks), tracks volume, and flags burnout risk.

Key principle: Volume matters, but tracked volume matters more. Every application should be traceable to a lead, a resume, a cover letter, and a Job ID.

---

## Step 7: Expand Past the JHS Master Doc

**Agent**: TSA J Search + TSA Port

Once the initial pipeline is flowing, expand your reach:

- **LinkedIn**: Active networking, not just job listings
- **Specialty job boards**: Industry-specific boards with less competition
- **Referrals**: A referred application is 4x more likely to result in a hire
- **Company career pages**: Many post roles on their own site first
- **Recruiters**: Engage staffing agencies in your field

**Portfolio development (TSA Port)**: As applications grow, build your GitHub profile, portfolio site, and LinkedIn presence. The portfolio compounds: every piece makes every application slightly stronger.

---

## Timeline

```
WEEK 1-2:  Steps 1-3 (Source, Build Resume, Create Master ATS List)
WEEK 3-4:  Steps 4-5 (Custom Materials, Color Scan Pipeline)
WEEK 5+:   Steps 6-7 (Mass Apply, Expand Sources, Build Portfolio)
ONGOING:   All agents active, pipeline flowing, lessons accumulating
```

By Week 3, you should be able to go from "new lead" to "application submitted" in under an hour per lead.
